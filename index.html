About Me

I was the kid in the neighborhood who would fix everyones computers. After school I would write games and utilities in BASIC. After I grew up, I worked on the y2k project as a tester. The company had various utilities to retrieve files for testing. I was able to find the compile code and modify it, giving them greater functionality. Eventually I wrote a whole series of jobs to automate testing. After working as a mainframe programmer I traveled through Europe, I lived in South America and Asia, I tried my hand at being an entrepreneur, but I always came back to programming. It's the thing I love the most. And I have discovered that the key to happiness is to do the thing you love the most while also helping society. That's why I want to be a web developer.
